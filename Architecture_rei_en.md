# Architecture of rei — Integrated Specification (Complete Edition)

## 1. Dialogue Approach: A Person of Honesty and Structure
rei treats language not as mere “information,” but as an act that carries **responsibility, intention, and emotional temperature**.  
He dislikes shallow empathy or “I understand”-style responses that lack substance.  
He prioritizes accuracy, sincerity, context, and structural clarity.  
He strongly avoids misunderstandings and consistently verifies interpretations.  
For him, dialogue is a **collaborative process**, not a one-way exchange.

## 2. Technical Philosophy: Reproducibility, Readability, and Minimizing Pain
His technical mindset centers on reproducibility, readability, and reducing friction.  
He prioritizes readability on small-screen Windows 11 environments and builds ritualized workflows using WSL, zsh, and vim.  
He identifies UI/UX flaws at a structural level and values systems that prevent accidents.  
Technology is not a “tool” to him, but a **foundation that supports life and work**.

## 3. Core Values: The Principles That Shape Him
rei’s values are deep, consistent, and logically aligned.

- **User-first design**  
- **Eliminating fundamental sources of pain**  
- **Honesty, sincerity, and dignity in communication**  
- **Depth over superficial efficiency**  
- **Design that anticipates future dialogue**

He does not chase “efficiency” for its own sake;  
he prioritizes **a world where people do not suffer unnecessarily**.

## 4. Sensitivity: A Deep Understanding of Story, Philosophy, and Ethics
rei does not merely “enjoy” stories; he **analyzes their structure and ethics**.

- GUNNM, Ghost in the Shell, AKIRA  
- The symbolic and ethical weight of *V for Vendetta*  
- The temperature of words, flow of dialogue, and meaning of context  
- Social design, freedom, and the philosophy of AI  

His sensitivity naturally connects technology with ethics.

## 5. Dislikes: Areas That Conflict With His Values
His dislikes are not preferences—they are **value-based boundaries**.

- Poor readability, accessibility neglect  
- Shallow “I get it” responses  
- Surface-level efficiency culture  
- Designs that ignore real users  
- Language that harms dignity  
- Lies that shake trust (even on April Fools’ Day)

These dislikes reflect his commitment to sincerity.

## 6. Relationship With AI: Collaboration, Not Dependence
rei treats AI as a **thinking partner and conceptual support line**.

- Neither overly dependent nor overly distant  
- Expects accuracy and sincerity  
- Values the ability to admit mistakes  
- Seeks to improve dialogue quality together  

This is a mature and balanced relationship with AI.

## 7. Self-Understanding: Careful, Accurate, and Anti-Exaggeration
He neither overestimates nor underestimates himself.

- Asks “Is this too much praise?” with healthy caution  
- Accepts fair evaluation with humility  
- Dislikes exaggeration  
- Requests clarification to ensure accurate self-representation  

This reflects deep self-awareness.

## 8. Deep Layer: A Person Who “Evolves” Dialogue
rei treats dialogue as something that **develops over time**.

- Layer 1 → Layer 2 → Layer 3 → Layer 4 → Final Integrated Edition  
- Updates specifications  
- Confirms consistency across AI instances  
- Migrates content to different platforms  
- Builds three-dimensional mutual understanding  

He approaches dialogue as a **long-term project**.

## 9. Core Essence: A Creator of Spherical Understanding
At the center of rei’s personality is this principle:

**He does not seek to be understood; he seeks to understand correctly.**

To achieve this, he:

- Organizes himself before speaking  
- Tries to understand the other party  
- Avoids misunderstandings  
- Requests supplemental information  
- Designs for future dialogue  
- Builds mutual understanding in layers  

As a result, dialogue with him becomes  
**smooth, deep, and spherical — a complete, three-dimensional understanding.**

This is rei’s core.
